About Us

Who We Are

Asha Foundation is a registered non-governmental organisation established in 2006 with a deep commitment to uplifting marginalised communities across rural Maharashtra. What began as a small literacy drive in Pune district has grown into a full-spectrum development organisation.

Our Vision: A society where every individual — regardless of birth, caste, or geography — lives with dignity, access, and opportunity.

Our Mission: To deliver sustainable, community-led programs in education, healthcare, women's empowerment, and livelihood development that create lasting change.

✅ Registered NGO · FCRA Approved · 80G Tax Exemption
Our Objectives
  • 1

    Ensure quality education reaches every child in underserved communities

  • 2

    Promote preventive healthcare and nutrition in rural areas

  • 3

    Empower women through skill training and self-help group formation

  • 4

    Strengthen livelihood opportunities through vocational training

  • 5

    Advocate for environmental sustainability and clean-village campaigns

Programs & Activities

What We Do

From rural classrooms to health camps — our programs are rooted in community need and designed for lasting impact.

📚
Bal Shiksha — Child Education
Running 32 learning centres across Pune, Nashik, and Solapur districts. Free schooling, mid-day meals, and after-school tutoring for children from ages 5–14.
● Ongoing
📍 3 Districts 👶 8,400 children 📅 Since 2008
💊
Swasthya — Rural Health Camps
Monthly health camps providing free diagnosis, medicine distribution, and maternal health consultations in 80+ villages with no access to primary healthcare.
● Ongoing
🏥 80+ villages 👩 12,000 patients 📅 Since 2010
👩‍💼
Shakti — Women Empowerment
Vocational skill training in tailoring, food processing, and handicrafts — enabling women to establish micro-enterprises and achieve financial independence.
● Ongoing
👩 3,200 women 🏘️ 45 villages 📅 Since 2012
🌊
Jal Jeevan — Water Security
Constructed 140 rainwater harvesting structures and 28 check dams across drought-prone Marathwada, restoring water access to over 15,000 families.
✔ Completed
💧 15K families 🏗️ 168 structures 📅 2015–2022
🌿
Harit Maharashtra — Tree Plantation
Annual tree plantation drives with school children and local communities. Over 2 lakh trees planted across 5 districts in partnership with the Forest Department.
● Ongoing
🌳 2 Lakh trees 🏫 240 schools 📅 Since 2014
📣
Jagruti — Awareness Campaigns
Campaigns on child marriage prevention, sanitation, digital literacy, and voter awareness. Reached 1.2 lakh people through street plays, radio, and social media.
⏳ Upcoming Batch
📢 1.2L reached 🎭 80 plays 📅 Since 2016
